🔧 Custom Frame Pack — Build Phases
Phase 1: Materials
Now – Apr 3 · Finalize every component before cutting
▼

🧱 Frame

Decide frame material — aluminum stay, HDPE sheet, carbon rod, or welded steel. Lock the spec before sourcing
Determine frame geometry: height relative to your torso length, hip shelf width, shoulder standoff
Source raw stock — have material in hand before Apr 3

🪢 Fabric & Hardware

Main body: 500D or 1000D Cordura, UHMWPE, or similar — decide weight vs. durability tradeoff
Hipbelt padding material — EVA foam thickness and density
Shoulder strap foam and webbing — 2" nylon or Dyneema webbing
All hardware sourced: buckles, D-rings, compression straps, load lifters, sternum strap

⚖️ Weight Budget

Set a target packed weight for the empty frame pack — SAC typically needs 35–45 lb loaded, so empty pack should be under 4 lb ideally
Map every component with estimated weight before fabricating

✅ Phase Gate

All materials in hand. Frame spec locked. Weight budget on paper. Ready to cut and sew.

Phase 2: Fabrication
Apr 3 – May 8 · Build the first functional version
▼

🔩 Frame Construction

Cut, shape, and finish the frame — file all edges, no sharp points that can cut fabric or skin
Drill or slot attachment points for shoulder straps and hipbelt — test fit before attaching anything
Mock up with just the frame before sewing anything — wear it, load it 20 lbs, walk 10 min

🧵 Sewing / Assembly

Build the main bag body first — don't add pockets or features yet, just the core volume
Attach frame to bag — test that frame doesn't shift under load
Attach shoulder straps and hipbelt — do a full fit with 20 lbs before finishing stitching
Add compression straps — you need the load to sit high and tight

🎯 Access & Organization

Think about what you need to access fast during the event — top lid, side pockets, hip belt pockets
Keep it simple: one main compartment, one top access, two hip pockets minimum

✅ Phase Gate

Prototype v1 complete and wearable. Can be loaded to 40 lbs. All straps attached and functional.

Phase 3: Field Testing
May 8 – Jun 27 · Break it before Wyoming does
▼

📋 Test Protocol

Every Saturday ruck is a pack test — log every hot spot, pressure point, and shift in your ruck log
Start at 40 lbs. By Week 14 you should be testing at event weight on mixed terrain
Test in the heat — pack performance changes when wet and sweaty

🔍 What to Evaluate Each Ruck

Hip transfer: is the hipbelt moving the weight onto your hips or dumping it on shoulders?
Frame contact: any rigid points digging into your back at mile 4+?
Load shift: does the pack move side-to-side on climbs? Tighten load lifters
Access: can you get what you need without taking the pack off?
Strap durability: check every buckle and bar tack after each session

🔧 Iteration Window

Weeks 11–16: fix anything structural — frame mods, strap repositioning, pocket changes — still OK
After Jun 27: NO structural changes. Only minor fit tuning allowed.

✅ Phase Gate

Pack has 6+ hours of loaded carry time. All major issues resolved. No new hot spots in last 2 sessions.

Phase 4: Lock
Jun 27 – Sep 6 · This is the pack. No changes.
▼

🔒 Freeze Date: June 27

No structural modifications after Jun 27 — you need 10+ weeks of rucking in the exact competition configuration
Your body adapts to this specific pack. Changing it late breaks that adaptation.

🧪 Final Validation (Jul–Aug)

Event sim weeks (Wks 18–23): pack takes 120 min+ ruck + obstacles every Saturday — this is your proof of concept
Identify and pre-treat any hot spots with moleskin, foam padding, or webbing adjustment
Pack a full mock gear list — practice your load configuration so it's muscle memory at Wyoming

🛠️ Maintenance Only

Inspect all bar tacks and seams monthly — resew anything showing wear before it fails
Check buckles and replace any that are cracking or stiff
Clean and re-waterproof fabric if needed — event is Wyoming, weather is a variable

✅ Phase Gate

Pack completes a 120 min ruck at event weight with zero issues. You trust it completely. It's done.
